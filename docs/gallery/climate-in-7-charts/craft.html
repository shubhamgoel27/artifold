Chart 01 / 07 — Atmosphere
01   Carbon Dioxide

The air is thicker. 425 parts per million. Higher than any moment in three million years.

Charles Keeling started measuring in 1958 from a Hawaiian volcano. The line has never gone down. Up 35 percent since the Industrial Revolution. Up 110 ppm in one human lifetime. The last time CO₂ was this high, Greenland was a forest.

+110
PPM since 1958
SOURCE: NOAA GLOBAL MONITORING LAB · MAUNA LOA OBSERVATORY
Chart 02 / 07 — Temperature
02   Surface Temperature

The planet ran a fever. +1.28°C in 2024. The hottest year in the instrumental record.

144 years of measurement. Every one of the ten hottest sits in the last decade. 2024 was the first calendar year to breach +1.5°C. The Paris ceiling was meant to be a wall. It is a turnstile.

+1.28°
Celsius · 2024
SOURCE: NASA GISS GISTEMP V4 · LAND-OCEAN TEMPERATURE INDEX
Chart 03 / 07 — Cryosphere
03   Arctic Sea Ice

The top of the world is melting. 2.7 million square kilometres lost from the September minimum since 1979.

In 1980 the September minimum was a frozen continent. Today it is a third smaller. 2024 placed seventh-lowest on record; every September of the last eighteen years sits below the 1980s average. Ships now sail where ice once stood.

−39%
since 1980
SOURCE: NSIDC SEA ICE INDEX V3 · SEPTEMBER MEAN EXTENT
Chart 04 / 07 — Oceans
04   Global Mean Sea Level

The ocean is rising. 106 millimetres higher than 1993. Four millimetres added every year.

Thermal expansion plus Greenland plus Antarctica. The rate has doubled. From 2.1 mm per year in the 1990s to 4.6 mm per year in the last decade. Miami, Jakarta, Dhaka, Lagos — do the math.

+106
millimetres
SOURCE: NASA JPL / U. COLORADO SATELLITE ALTIMETRY · TOPEX-JASON
Chart 05 / 07 — Disasters
05   Billion-Dollar Events · U.S.

The bills are coming due. 28 billion-dollar disasters in 2024. The 1980s averaged three.

NOAA only counts disasters costing a billion dollars or more, inflation-adjusted. The U.S. now averages one such event every fortnight. Hurricanes, derechos, wildfires, floods. The atmosphere holds more energy. It spends it.

9.3×
more than the 1980s
SOURCE: NOAA NCEI · U.S. BILLION-DOLLAR WEATHER & CLIMATE DISASTERS
Chart 06 / 07 — Emitters
06   Top Ten Emitters · 2023

Ten countries. 25.9 gigatonnes. Two thirds of the world's CO₂.

China emits more CO₂ than the United States, India and Russia combined. The United States, per capita, still doubles China. Ten flags. Twenty-six billion tonnes. One sky. Nobody decarbonises alone.

Chart 07 / 07 — The Window
07   Remaining Carbon Budget

For a coin-flip at 1.5°C: 250 gigatonnes left. We spend 37 per year. The math is brutal.

The IPCC's central estimate. Fifty-fifty odds. Half the chance of a coin. Every gigatonne sooner buys a degree later. Every year of delay shaves a year off the window. There is no extension.

years at current rate
