A quick visual tour of the most important indicators showing how Earth's climate is changing. All figures are real-world approximations drawn from sources such as NOAA, NASA GISS, IPCC, and the NSIDC.

CO2 has risen from 315 ppm to about 425 ppm — a 35% increase in just 66 years, and a level not seen on Earth for over 3 million years. Source: NOAA Mauna Loa.

2. Global Temperature Anomaly (1880–2024)

Earth's average surface temperature is now about +1.2°C above the 1880 baseline, with most warming occurring after 1975. Source: NASA GISTEMP.

6. Ocean Heat Content (0–2000m, 1960–2024)

More than 90% of excess heat trapped by greenhouse gases has been absorbed by the oceans, with content rising ~400 zettajoules since 1960. Source: NOAA NCEI.
