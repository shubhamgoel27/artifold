Recommendation: 989 20th Street (Dogpatch)

For a couple optimizing a Mission Bay commute, sunny mornings, and an EV-friendly daily routine, 989 20th Street is the best fit. It wins on the three highest-weighted criteria (commute, morning sun, dog-friendliness) while being the cheapest all-in by ~$300–$1,000/month.

  • Commute math: a 6-minute bike or 12-minute walk eliminates Muni dependency entirely — a meaningful daily quality-of-life win versus the two SoMa-side options.
  • Microclimate: Dogpatch sits in one of the city's reliable sun pockets; NEMA in particular loses on grey-morning frequency.
  • Total cost: ~$5,250 all-in vs ~$6,225 at 400 Beale frees ~$11,700/year — enough to fund travel or a future move-up.
  • Dog logistics: Crane Cove + Esprit Park beat any rooftop run; no breed/weight limits removes future risk.

Consider 400 Beale only if the view premium and Embarcadero lifestyle outweigh ~$1k/mo and a longer commute. NEMA is the right pick only if the amenity stack (two gyms, pools, courts) is used heavily — otherwise the marine layer and Mid-Market friction outweigh it.
