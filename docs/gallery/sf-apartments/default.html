Recommendation

Go with 989 20th Street (Dogpatch)

It wins on the two things that quietly compound over a year: a sub-15-minute bike commute to the Mission Bay office and morning sun without fighting the western shadow. Esprit Park being a block away is a daily quality-of-life upgrade for the dog that NEMA cannot fake with an indoor pet spa.

Why not the others:

  1. 400 Beale — the bay view is glorious for 6 weeks, then becomes wallpaper. You'd pay ~$1,100/mo more for amenities that don't move the needle, and the Mission Bay commute is meaningfully slower.
  2. NEMA — the gym and amenities are tempting, but the west-facing unit kills morning light, the dog situation is genuinely worse, and the Mid-Market block still has rough edges that make evening walks feel like a chore.

Action: Submit application to Trumark on 989 20th this week. Lock the EV-included parking spot before it moves. Keep NEMA as a fallback only if Dogpatch lease falls through.
