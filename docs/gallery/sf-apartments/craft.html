A decision in three rents

Three apartments,
one signature.

The question

All three are good. None of them are the right answer for everyone. The frame that matters: which place do we want to come home to every weekday at 7pm, knowing the commute is paid for either way and the rent delta is real but not life-changing.

Option 01 · Recommended

989 20th Street

Dogpatch
$5,000 / mo

Quiet residential block on the eastern edge of the city. Two blocks to Esprit Park, ten minutes to Mission Bay by bike, fast on-ramp to 280 for weekends out.

Option 02

400 Beale Street

Rincon Hill
$5,800 / mo

High-floor bay views, Embarcadero at the doorstep. The most "skyline-postcard" of the three. You pay $800/mo for the view and the address.

Option 03

NEMA

SoMa · 8th & Market
$5,200 / mo

The amenity stack is the product: two pools, a real gym, dog spa, co-working. Trade is the corner — 8th & Market is the busiest seam of the three.

The call

Take 989 20th.
The math, the dog, the mornings.

Dogpatch wins on four of the criteria that bite daily — commute, sun, dog, parking — and loses only on amenities Annu and I have never paid for. The other two options are buying a feature we already get cheaper somewhere else.

400 Beale is the postcard. It's $13.8k a year for a view we already see from the Embarcadero on any walk. The commute is worse than it looks on a map and the dog routine is a daily small friction.

NEMA is the most rational on paper and the most uncomfortable in person. The 8th & Market block is the variable nothing else fixes — and the amenity premium evaporates if we never give up Equinox.

Why not Beale
Pay $13.8k/yr for a view you already get on the daily walk; the dog routine gets harder.
Why not NEMA
The corner is the product you can't unbundle. Amenities don't fix the walk to the door at 11pm.
What we'd add
Equinox Mission Bay ($230/mo). Even with it, Dogpatch is the cheapest option.
What to verify on tour
Unit faces east & not blocked by the new 1100 25th build. Charger stall & current speed. Quiet hours enforcement.
If Dogpatch falls through

Second choice — NEMA

Pick NEMA only if we commit to using the gym. The amenity savings ($230/mo) cancel the rent premium ($200/mo) and the building does the lifting. The walk to the door stays the cost of admission.

Pass on Beale

Not at $6,150 all-in. Revisit only if the rent comes down below $5,400 and they throw in parking. Otherwise it's a postcard you'll see twice a year.
