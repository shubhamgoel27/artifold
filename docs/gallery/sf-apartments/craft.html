The Contenders

Three buildings, three theories of the city.

01

989 20th Street

Dogpatch · ~$5,000 / mo · 2018 build

A newer mid-rise tucked off Indiana, three blocks from Esprit Park. Quiet at night in a way most of SF isn't. Coffee at Réveille, dinner at Piccino, freeway in under a minute when you actually need to leave the city.

Trade-off: nightlife is thin, and the 22-Fillmore is your only real Muni lifeline.

The "we want a neighborhood, not an address" option.

02

400 Beale Street

Rincon Hill · ~$5,800 / mo · high-rise, 30+ floors

Tower living with a real bay view from the upper floors. The Embarcadero is a five-minute walk; the Ferry Building farmer's market becomes a Saturday default. BART, Muni, and the bridge ramp all within blocks.

Trade-off: the highest sticker, the smallest unit per dollar, and the building lobby is the only "outdoors" until you cross Folsom.

The "view tax is real and we accept it" option.

03

NEMA

SoMa · 8th & Market · ~$5,200 / mo · large complex

The amenity stack is genuinely best-in-class: 12,000 sq ft gym, two pools, rooftop, screening room, co-working lounges. Mid-Market is finally turning the corner with new ground-floor retail and a heavier daytime crowd.

Trade-off: the block itself still has rough edges after dark, and the complex is large enough that nobody knows their neighbors.

The "the apartment IS the neighborhood" option.

Verdict

Sign at 989 20th.

It wins on the three axes that compound daily — commute, morning light, and Esprit Park as the dog's living room — and it does so at the lowest all-in cost. The amenity gap versus NEMA is real, but pools and rooftops are weekend wins; they don't reshape a Tuesday.

  1. Commute is the highest-leverage variable. A 14-minute bike to Mission Bay buys back ~80 hours a year versus the SoMa or Rincon options. That's the trip that actually compounds.
  2. Morning sun is a constraint, not a preference. NEMA's tower shadow and Beale's east-glare-then-nothing both fail it. 989's SE exposure is the only one that holds through 10am.
  3. The dog vetoes Beale. A 35-lb cap and no relief space is a daily friction tax. Esprit Park across the street is the inverse.
  4. Save the $1,000/mo delta versus Beale. Twelve months of that gap funds the kitchen renovation we've been talking about, or a real travel year.

Hold NEMA as backup if 989 falls through — its amenity stack at $5,200 is the second-best value. Pass on Beale unless the bay view is the single thing we'd most regret not having.
